Iced Cappuccino£1.90

Also known as a "frothy cappuccino," is a cold coffee beverage made by combining espresso, milk, and foam, served over ice.

It is a variation of the traditional cappuccino, which is a hot espresso-based drink made with steamed milk and milk foam.

To make an iced cappuccino, the espresso is first brewed and allowed to cool, then combined with cold milk and topped with a thick layer of foam, typically made from frothed milk.

The drink is then poured over ice, creating a refreshing, creamy, and frothy coffee beverage that is perfect for hot weather.

It can be served plain or with flavor syrups and toppings such as whipped cream or chocolate shavings.

Matcha Cookies

Matcha cookies are a type of cookie that is made with matcha powder, which is a fine green tea powder that is commonly used in Japanese cuisine.

Matcha cookies are often light and crispy, with a delicate flavor that is slightly sweet and slightly bitter.

They can be made with a variety of ingredients, such as butter, sugar, flour, and eggs, and may include other flavors such as vanilla or chocolate.

Matcha cookies are popular in Japan and have become increasingly popular in other parts of the world due to the growing interest in matcha and its health benefits.
